Hadith: Taqwa, Repentance and Beautiful Character

اتَّقِ اللهَ حيثُما كنتَ ، وأتبِعِ السَّيِّئةَ الحسَنةَ تَمْحُهَا ، وخالِقِ النَّاسَ بخُلُقٍ حَسنٍ

"Be mindful of Allah wherever you are, follow a bad deed with a good deed which will erase it, and treat people with beautiful character."

Collected by Tirmidhi • Narrated by Mu'adh ibn Jabal (may Allah be pleased with him)

Part 1 · Overview

The Three Main Messages

This hadith contains three connected instructions that together form a complete way of living as a Muslim: Taqwa (consciousness of Allah), repentance through good deeds after sin, and beautiful character in dealing with people.

  • “Be mindful of Allah wherever you are” points to Taqwa.
  • “Follow a bad deed with a good deed which will erase it” teaches practical repentance.
  • “Treat people with beautiful character” shows the social side of faith.

Key idea: This hadith guides our relationship with Allah, our response when we sin, and our relationship with other people.

Part 3 · Three Panels

At a Glance: Taqwa, Repentance, Character

Panel 1: Taqwa

Remembering Allah in private and public, in joy and difficulty. Taqwa makes a person careful with what they say, look at, and do.

Panel 2: After a Sin

When a mistake happens, do not give up. Turn back to Allah, feel regret, seek forgiveness, and do a good deed to repair the damage.

Panel 3: Beautiful Character

Real faith appears in how we treat others. Kindness, honesty, patience, and helpfulness are all part of worship.

Part 4 · Taqwa

Taqwa: Being Mindful of Allah

Meaning of Taqwa

Taqwa means to protect yourself from Allah’s anger by obeying His commands and staying away from His prohibitions. It is a constant awareness that Allah sees and knows everything we do.

A person with Taqwa asks before any action: “Is this pleasing to Allah?” This inner question guides their choices in school, online, at home, and with friends.

Taqwa “Wherever You Are”

  • At home: respecting parents, being helpful, avoiding rude speech.
  • At school: being honest in exams, not cheating or bullying.
  • Online: not sharing haram content, not mocking or insulting others.
  • Alone: avoiding sins even when nobody can see, because Allah always sees.

Reminder: Taqwa is not only for the masjid. It is for every place, every time, and every situation.

Part 5 · Repentance and Good Deeds

Following a Bad Deed with a Good Deed

Why We Need Repentance

Every human being makes mistakes. The difference between a good believer and a careless person is that the believer returns to Allah quickly after sin and tries to repair what was damaged.

Steps of Repentance

  • Realise and admit that what you did was wrong.
  • Feel genuine regret in your heart.
  • Ask Allah for forgiveness with sincere du‘a.
  • Stop the sin immediately.
  • Plan not to repeat it and take practical steps to avoid it.
  • Do good deeds to wipe out the bad deed.

Important: The hadith does not give us permission to sin on purpose. It teaches how to respond correctly when we do fall into sin, which is a natural part of being human.

Examples of Following Bad with Good

  • You speak harshly to your parents. After regret and apology, you show extra kindness, help with chores, and make du‘a for them.
  • You wasted time and missed a prayer. You make it up as soon as possible, ask forgiveness, and try to pray the next prayers on time and with more focus.
  • You insulted a classmate. You apologise, stop the mockery, speak kindly afterwards, and defend them if others tease them.

These good deeds do not erase the sin by themselves, but they are part of sincere repentance and a sign that you truly want to change.

Part 6 · Beautiful Character

Husn al-Akhlaq: Beautiful Character

What is Beautiful Character?

Beautiful character means dealing with people in the best possible way: with honesty, patience, kindness, and fairness. It includes how we talk, how we react when we are angry, and how we treat those who are weaker than us.

Examples of Beautiful Character

  • Speaking politely to younger siblings and not looking down on them.
  • Smiling and greeting others first, even if you do not know them well.
  • Keeping your promises and being trustworthy with secrets and belongings.
  • Being gentle when correcting others instead of shouting.
  • Helping classmates who struggle with studies without making fun of them.
  • Controlling your anger and not taking revenge for small matters.

Prophetic teaching: The Prophet said that the best of people are those with the best character, and that good character will be very heavy on the scale of deeds on the Day of Judgement.

Link Between Character and Faith

This hadith links worship of Allah with how we treat people. A person cannot claim strong faith if they constantly hurt others with their words and actions. Good character is one of the clearest signs that Taqwa and repentance are real inside the heart.
