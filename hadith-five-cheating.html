Section 1

The Hadeeth Text

عَنْ أَبِي هُرَيْرَةَ أَنَّ رَسُولَ اللهِ ﷺ مَرَّ فِي السُّوقِ عَلَى حُبْرَةِ طَعَامٍ ، أَدْخَلَ يَدَهُ فِيهَا، فَنَالَتْ أَصَابِعُهُ بَلَلًا، فَقَالَ: مَا هَذَا يَا صَاحِبَ الطَّعَامِ؟ قَالَ: يَا رَسُولَ اللَّهُ أَصَابَتْهُ السَّمَاءُ. قَالَ: «أَفَلَا جَعَلْتَهُ فَوْقَ الطَّعَامِ حَتَّى يَرَاهُ النَّاسُ؟» وَقَالَ: «مَنْ غَشَّنَا فَلَيْسَ مِنَّا»

"Abu Hurayrah reported that Allaah's Messenger ﷺ passed by a pile of food and stuck his hand into it. When he found it wet, he asked its owner, 'What is this?' The trader replied, 'Rain fell on it, O Messenger of Allaah.' The Prophet then said, 'Why didn't you put it on top so that the people could see it? Whoever cheats us is not of us.'"

Collected by: Imaam Muslim, at-Tirmidhee, and Abu Daawood

Section 3

The Story — Step by Step

1

🕌 The Prophet ﷺ Walks Through the Market

One day, the Prophet Muhammad ﷺ was walking through the marketplace (السوق). Markets were busy, public places — the centre of commerce and trade in early Islamic Madinah.

2

🌾 He Notices a Pile of Grain

The Prophet ﷺ passed by a large heap of wheat or food grain (حُبْرَة طَعَامٍ). Something caught his attention — perhaps the dry outer appearance concealing something underneath.

3

✋ He Puts His Hand Into the Pile

The Prophet ﷺ reached in and inserted his blessed hand deep into the pile. His fingers felt wetness — the grain inside was damp and potentially damaged, while the surface appeared dry and fine.

4

❓ He Questions the Trader

The Prophet ﷺ asked the owner: "What is this, O owner of the food?" Note: he already knew the answer — but he asked publicly to expose the practice and make the man an example to all traders in the market.

5

🌧️ The Trader's Excuse

The trader replied: "Rain fell on it, O Messenger of Allaah." The grain got wet the previous night. Instead of telling buyers, he hid the wet grain under dry grain on top — a deliberate act of deception.

6

⚠️ The Prophet ﷺ Corrects Him

The Prophet said: "Why didn't you put the wet grain on top so that the people could see it?" This teaches that a seller MUST disclose defects. Hiding damage is cheating — regardless of the reason.

7

📣 The General Warning

Addressing all traders present, the Prophet ﷺ declared: "Whoever cheats us is NOT of us." This powerful statement means a cheater has distanced themselves from true Islamic behaviour and the Muslim community.

Section 4

Key Lessons from This Hadeeth

1

Honesty in All Dealings

A Muslim must be truthful in business, school, and daily life. Hiding faults to gain an advantage is cheating.

2

Transparency is Obligatory

Sellers must disclose defects. Students must not hide cheating. Employees must not falsify reports. Transparency protects everyone.

3

Allah is Always Watching

Even if no teacher or authority catches you, Allaah sees every act. Every sin is recorded and will be accounted for.

4

Cheating Harms the Community

A cheating trader destroys trust in the marketplace. A cheating student undermines fairness for honest students.

5

Helping Others Cheat is Also a Sin

Providing answers, allowing copying, or covering for a cheater makes you equally responsible. True help means stopping them.

6

The Prophet ﷺ was Al-Ameen

Even before prophethood, his tribe called him "Al-Ameen" (الأمين — The Trustworthy). Honesty was his defining quality.

7

Cheaters Lose the Prophet's Companionship

"Not of us" (لَيْسَ مِنَّا) is a severe warning — it means the cheater has abandoned the character and community of Muslims.

8

Honest Sales are Blessed

The Prophet ﷺ said: "If buyer and seller are honest, their sale is blessed. If they deceive, the blessing is removed." Honesty attracts barakah.

🌟

Al-Ameen — The Trustworthy

Long before the Prophet Muhammad ﷺ received revelation, the people of Makkah — including his enemies — called him الأَمِين (Al-Ameen — The Trustworthy) because of his unmatched honesty. They trusted him with their valuables and secrets.

This shows that honesty is a universal virtue — and for a Muslim, it is even more important because it connects to our belief in Allaah and the Day of Judgement. Our character must reflect our faith.

Section 5

Supporting Qur'anic Verses

Surah Al-Maa'idah (5):2 — On Helping in Good

وَتَعَاوَنُوا عَلَى الْبِرِّ وَالتَّقْوَىٰ وَلَا تَعَاوَنُوا عَلَى الْإِثْمِ وَالْعُدْوَانِ

"Help one another in righteousness and piety, but do not help one another in sin and transgression."
Soorah Al-Maa'idah (5): Verse 2

This verse is directly connected to cheating. When a student passes answers to another, or when a trader hides defects with the help of an assistant, both are guilty. Allaah commands cooperation in goodness (البِرّ) and piety (التَّقوى) — but strictly forbids cooperation in sin (الإثم). Helping someone cheat is itself a sin.

Surah Al-Zalzalah (99):8 — Every Deed is Recorded

وَمَن يَعْمَلْ مِثْقَالَ ذَرَّةٍ شَرًّا يَرَهُۥ

"And whoever does an atom's weight of evil will see it."
Soorah Al-Zalzalah (99): Verse 8

This powerful verse reminds us that nothing escapes Allaah's record. Even the smallest act of cheating — glancing at a classmate's answer, sending a WhatsApp message during an exam, or hiding a defective product — will be presented to us on the Day of Judgement. There is no escape from divine accountability.

Surah Al-Maa'idah (5):1 — Fulfill Your Obligations

يَٰٓأَيُّهَا ٱلَّذِينَ ءَامَنُوٓا۟ أَوْفُوا۟ بِٱلْعُقُودِ

"O you who have believed, fulfil your contracts (obligations)."
Soorah Al-Maa'idah (5): Verse 1

When a student sits for an exam, there is an implicit agreement (عَقْد) with the school, the teacher, and society — to demonstrate their own knowledge honestly. When a trader sells goods, there is a contract with the buyer to provide what was agreed upon. Fulfilling these obligations is an Islamic duty. Cheating violates them.

Section 6

Real & Modern Life Examples

The Prophet's ﷺ lesson about the grain trader applies directly to our lives today. Cheating takes many forms — some obvious, some subtle. Islam considers all of them forbidden.

📝 Exam Cheating
Hiding a "cheat sheet" in your pencil case, looking at a classmate's paper during an exam, or sending photos of exam questions via WhatsApp to friends in another class.
⚠️ This is haraam — equivalent to the wet grain hidden under dry grain.
🛒 Online Selling
Selling a secondhand phone on Shopee or Carousell without mentioning a cracked screen, or posting only good-angle photos while hiding dents or damage.
⚠️ Like putting wet grain under dry grain — hiding the defect.
🤳 Social Media Deception
Posting heavily edited photos to sell products while the actual items look nothing like the pictures. Common in food delivery, fashion, and skincare businesses.
⚠️ Deliberately misleading customers is a form of ghish (غِشّ).
💼 Workplace Dishonesty
An employee submitting a falsified attendance record, claiming overtime hours not actually worked, or padding an expense report with personal costs.
⚠️ This betrays the trust (amanah) of the employer.
🎮 Online Gaming / Apps
Using hacks, mods, or bots in competitive online games to gain unfair advantages over other honest players. Faking screenshots of achievements.
⚠️ Cheating in games trains dishonest habits.
🚗 Buying / Selling Cars
A used car seller who rolls back the odometer (tampering with mileage readings) or covers up engine problems with paint so the buyer doesn't know the real condition.
⚠️ Exactly like hiding the wet grain — forbidden in Islam.
🚫

Helping Others Cheat is NOT "Kindness"

Some students say: "I'm just helping my friend!" — but the Prophet ﷺ was asked the same question. He replied: "Help your brother by PREVENTING him" from doing wrong. The only true help is stopping someone from sinning. Giving answers, allowing copying, or covering up cheating makes you equally responsible before Allaah.

Section 7

Key Vocabulary

الغِشّ
Al-Ghish (Cheating / Deception)
Deliberately hiding the truth or defects to gain an unfair advantage. The main topic of this hadeeth.
الأَمَانَة
Al-Amaanah (Trustworthiness)
The quality of being honest, reliable, and faithful. The opposite of cheating. A core Islamic virtue.
الأَمِين
Al-Ameen (The Trustworthy)
The title given to Prophet Muhammad ﷺ by the Quraysh tribe, even before he became a prophet.
السُّوق
Al-Souq (The Market)
The marketplace or bazaar — where the incident of this hadeeth took place. The public setting made the lesson more powerful.
الصِّدْق
As-Sidq (Honesty / Truthfulness)
Being truthful in speech and action. Highly praised in Islam; the Prophet ﷺ called it a path to Paradise.
البَرَكَة
Al-Barakah (Blessing)
Divine blessing in one's wealth, time, and deeds. Honest transactions are blessed; dishonest ones lose their barakah.
التَّعَاوُن
At-Ta'aawun (Cooperation)
Working together. The Qur'an commands cooperation in goodness (al-birr), but forbids cooperation in sin (ithm).
الإِثْم
Al-Ithm (Sin)
An act that is forbidden by Allaah. Cheating is a sin; helping someone cheat is also a sin.
