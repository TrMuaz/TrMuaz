Section 01

Definition of Hadeeth

What is Hadeeth? 📖

Hadeeth (حديث) means "a report" or "a story". In Islam, hadeeth is anything that Prophet Muhammad ﷺ said, did, or approved.

الحديث

It is a record of the Prophet's ﷺ words and actions, taught by his companions (sahabah) and passed down carefully to us today.

Easy Meaning ✨

Quran = the Words of Allah. Hadeeth = the words and actions of Prophet Muhammad ﷺ.
Section 02

The Purpose of Hadeeth in Islam

Why do we need Hadeeth? 🎯

The Quran is our main guide. But sometimes the Quran gives a rule in short words. Hadeeth explains and shows us how to follow it.

  • 1.To explain the Quran. Example: Quran says "pray", hadeeth shows us how to pray.
  • 2.To teach Islamic rules not mentioned in detail in the Quran.
  • 3.To give us a good example from the Prophet's ﷺ life.
  • 4.To teach us good manners (akhlaq), worship, and how to treat others.
  • 5.To protect the religion from mistakes and wrong ideas.
Section 03

The Parts: Sanad & Matan

Every hadeeth has two parts 🔗

Sanad = the chain of people who told the hadeeth (like a list of narrators).

Matan = the text or the actual words of the hadeeth.

Section 04

Types of Hadeeth

Hadeeth has three main types, based on what came from the Prophet ﷺ: his sayings, his actions, and his approvals.

Type 1 · Qawli

🗣️ Saying (Qawli)

Words that the Prophet ﷺ said with his mouth.

"The best of you is the one who learns the Quran and teaches it."— Sahih Bukhari
Type 2 · Fi'li

🤲 Action (Fi'li)

Things the Prophet ﷺ did, and the companions saw and reported.

The way the Prophet ﷺ prayed (standing, ruku, sujood) — the companions watched and then taught us.— Reported by many sahabah
Type 3 · Taqriri

✅ Approval (Taqriri)

Things the Prophet ﷺ saw or heard a companion do, and he did not stop it. His silence = approval.

The companions ate dhabb (a type of lizard) in front of the Prophet ﷺ. He did not eat it, but he did not stop them either — so it is allowed.— Sahih Bukhari
Section 05

Famous Books of Hadeeth

After the Prophet ﷺ passed away, great scholars travelled and collected hadeeth. They wrote them in books so we can read them today.

The six most trusted books are called Kutub al-Sittah (The Six Books) 📚.

Sahih al-Bukhari

Imam al-Bukhari
The most authentic book of hadeeth.

Sahih Muslim

Imam Muslim
Second most authentic book.

Sunan Abu Dawud

Imam Abu Dawud

Jami' at-Tirmidhi

Imam at-Tirmidhi
Grades each hadeeth's strength.

Sunan an-Nasa'i

Imam an-Nasa'i

Sunan ibn Majah

Imam Ibn Majah
Completes the Six Books.
Section 06

Levels of Hadeeth (by Sanad)

Not every hadeeth is the same strength. Scholars check the sanad (chain of narrators) to decide the level. If the people in the chain are truthful and have strong memory, the hadeeth is strong.

1

⭐ Sahih — Authentic

The chain is strong and all narrators are trustworthy. We must accept it.

2

✅ Hasan — Good

The chain is good but a little weaker than sahih. Still accepted.

3

⚠️ Da'if — Weak

There is a problem in the chain. Scholars are careful when using it.

4

❌ Mawdu' — Fabricated

Made up. Not really from the Prophet ﷺ. We must not use it.
