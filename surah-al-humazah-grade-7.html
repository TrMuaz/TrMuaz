Surah Al-Humazah

Grade 7 lesson notes with simple tafsir, reflection, and quiz.

Quick focus

Main warning: Do not mock people, backbite, or become proud because of wealth.

Grade 7 Qur'an studies

Learn how Allah warns against backbiting, mocking, and love of wealth ⚖️

Surah Al-Humazah is a short Makki surah with 9 verses. It teaches us to protect our tongue, our manners, and our heart.

9 ayat Makki surah Character building

Learning goals

  • Understand the main meaning of Surah Al-Humazah.
  • Identify bad behaviors mentioned in the surah.
  • Explain what Al-Hutamah means in simple words.
  • Apply the message in school, family, and online life.

Think before you speak: words can hurt hearts even when no wound can be seen.

Surah overview

Ayat and simple meanings

Read each verse, then focus on the key idea in easy English.

1
وَيْلٌ لِّكُلِّ هُمَزَةٍ لُّمَزَةٍ

Meaning: Destruction and warning for every person who insults and mocks others.

2
ٱلَّذِى جَمَعَ مَالًا وَعَدَّدَهُۥ

Meaning: The one who keeps collecting wealth and counting it again and again.

3
يَحْسَبُ أَنَّ مَالَهُۥٓ أَخْلَدَهُۥ

Meaning: He thinks his wealth will make him live forever or protect him from loss.

4
كَلَّا ۖ لَيُنۢبَذَنَّ فِى ٱلْحُطَمَةِ

Meaning: Never. He will be thrown into Al-Hutamah, the crushing fire.

5-7
وَمَآ أَدْرَىٰكَ مَا ٱلْحُطَمَةُ ۝ نَارُ ٱللَّهِ ٱلْمُوقَدَةُ ۝ ٱلَّتِى تَطَّلِعُ عَلَى ٱلْأَفْـِٔدَةِ

Meaning: Al-Hutamah is Allah's blazing fire that reaches the hearts.

8-9
إِنَّهَا عَلَيْهِم مُّؤْصَدَةٌ ۝ فِى عَمَدٍ مُّمَدَّدَةٍۭ

Meaning: The fire will be closed over them in stretched columns.

Simple tafsir

What the surah is teaching us

1. Humazah and Lumazah

The surah begins with a strong warning against people who hurt others by mocking, insulting, or speaking badly about them. Scholars explain that this can happen through words, facial expressions, gestures, or actions.

2. Wealth can become a test

The problem is not having money. The problem is becoming obsessed with it, counting it proudly, and thinking it gives safety, status, or forever life.

3. Al-Hutamah

Al-Hutamah means the crushing or breaking fire. It shows a punishment that crushes pride and reaches the heart, because the sins started from a diseased heart.

4. Why the heart is mentioned

The surah mentions hearts to teach that sins like arrogance, cruelty, and mocking begin inside. A clean tongue needs a clean heart.

Student summary

This surah teaches that bullying, gossip, backbiting, and showing off wealth are serious sins. A Muslim should speak kindly, respect others, and remember that only Allah gives true safety.

Life lessons

How to apply it today

At school 🎒

Do not laugh at classmates, create hurtful nicknames, or spread rumors.

Online 📱

Avoid nasty comments, screenshots for gossip, and posting to shame others.

At home 🏡

Speak with adab, especially when angry, jealous, or upset.

Signs to avoid

  • Mocking someone's looks, accent, clothes, or mistakes.
  • Talking about people behind their back.
  • Feeling better than others because of money or status.
  • Forgetting the Hereafter because of dunya.

Better habits

  • Use words that heal, not words that break.
  • Make du'a for people instead of insulting them.
  • Share wealth and blessings with gratitude.
  • Remember that Allah sees what is in the heart.

References

Major tafsir used for this lesson

These notes simplify classical and modern commentary for Grade 7 students.

Tafsir Ibn Kathir

Used for the explanation that humazah and lumazah refer to insulting, belittling, and fault-finding through speech, actions, eyes, or gestures, and that Al-Hutamah is a crushing fire that reaches the hearts.

Maarif al-Qur'an

Used for the summary that the surah warns against three major wrongs: backbiting, deriding people, and selfish hoarding of wealth.

Classroom note

The wording here is intentionally made simple and age-appropriate so students can understand the message and apply it in daily life.
