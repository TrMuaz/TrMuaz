Grade 9 Islamic Studies

The Format of the Prophet’s Prayer ﷺ

Study these 13 sections carefully. Open one section at a time, read the explanation, learn the recitations, and pay attention to the names of the prayer positions. Your aim is not only to memorise the words, but also to understand the correct manner of the salaah as taught by the Prophet ﷺ.

Instruction to students: revise section by section, compare the positions, and practise the recitations with correct pronunciation.

The Prophet ﷺ began the prayer with the opening takbeer. He would sometimes raise his hands before the takbeer, sometimes with it, and sometimes just after it. His hands were raised to the shoulders or parallel to the earlobes, with the fingers kept natural.

Key points

  • Begin the prayer by saying the opening takbeer.
  • Raise the hands calmly and properly.
  • This takbeer marks the start of the salaah.

Recitation

اللّٰهُ أَكْبَرُ
Allāhu Akbar
Allah is the Greatest.

After the opening takbeer, the Prophet ﷺ placed his right hand over his left hand. Reports mention placing the right hand on the back of the left hand, wrist, and forearm, and also placing the hands on the chest while standing.

Key points

  • The right hand is placed over the left hand.
  • The standing posture should be calm and humble.
  • This is part of the dignity and discipline of the prayer.

Reported meaning

The text explains that the Prophets were ordered to place the right hand over the left in salaah. This shows that the posture is not random; it is part of the taught form of prayer.

When standing in prayer, the Prophet ﷺ lowered his head and looked towards the place of sujood. He forbade looking around and warned against actions that distract a person during prayer. The purpose is to increase khushoo‘, concentration, and reward.

Key points

  • Keep your gaze towards the place of sujood.
  • Avoid looking around or upwards.
  • Do not pray while distracted by food or a pressing need to relieve yourself.
  • Pray as though it may be your farewell prayer.

Hadeeth meaning

The lesson text mentions that looking around in prayer is something Shaytaan steals from the worshipper’s salaah. It also teaches that only part of a prayer may be recorded for a person depending on the level of concentration.

Before reciting Surah al-Fatihah, the Prophet ﷺ would sometimes say different opening supplications. These du‘aas are recited quietly. More than one authentic wording is reported, so a student may learn any reliable form.

Recitation 1

سُبْحَانَكَ اللّٰهُمَّ وَبِحَمْدِكَ وَتَبَارَكَ اسْمُكَ وَتَعَالَى جَدُّكَ وَلَا إِلٰهَ غَيْرُكَ
Subḥānakallāhumma wa biḥamdik, wa tabārakasmuk, wa ta‘ālā jadduk, wa lā ilāha ghayruk.
Glory and praise be to You, O Allah. Blessed is Your name, exalted is Your majesty, and there is no god besides You.

Recitation 2

اللّٰهُ أَكْبَرُ كَبِيرًا وَالْحَمْدُ لِلّٰهِ كَثِيرًا وَسُبْحَانَ اللّٰهِ بُكْرَةً وَأَصِيلًا
Allāhu akbaru kabīrā, wal-ḥamdu lillāhi kathīrā, wa subḥānallāhi bukratan wa aṣīlā.
Allah is truly the Greatest, praise belongs abundantly to Allah, and glory be to Allah morning and evening.

Recitation 3

اللّٰهُمَّ بَاعِدْ بَيْنِي وَبَيْنَ خَطَايَايَ كَمَا بَاعَدْتَ بَيْنَ الْمَشْرِقِ وَالْمَغْرِبِ، اللّٰهُمَّ نَقِّنِي مِنْ خَطَايَايَ كَمَا يُنَقَّى الثَّوْبُ الْأَبْيَضُ مِنَ الدَّنَسِ، اللّٰهُمَّ اغْسِلْنِي مِنْ خَطَايَايَ بِالْمَاءِ وَالثَّلْجِ وَالْبَرَدِ
Allāhumma bā‘id baynī wa bayna khaṭāyāya kamā bā‘adta bayna al-mashriqi wal-maghrib. Allāhumma naqqinī min khaṭāyāya kamā yunaqqā ath-thawbul-abyaḍu min ad-danas. Allāhumma ighsilnī min khaṭāyāya bil-mā’i wath-thalji wal-barad.
O Allah, distance me from my sins as You have distanced the east from the west. O Allah, cleanse me of my sins as a white garment is cleansed of dirt. O Allah, wash me of my sins with water, snow, and hail.

After the opening du‘aa, the Prophet ﷺ would seek refuge in Allah, then say the basmalah quietly and recite Surah al-Fatihah carefully, pausing at the end of each verse. After al-Fatihah, he would recite another surah or some verses from the Qur’an.

Ta‘awwudh

أَعُوذُ بِاللّٰهِ مِنَ الشَّيْطَانِ الرَّجِيمِ
A‘ūdhu billāhi min ash-shayṭānir-rajīm.
I seek refuge in Allah from the accursed Shaytaan.

Basmalah

بِسْمِ اللّٰهِ الرَّحْمٰنِ الرَّحِيمِ
Bismillāhir-Raḥmānir-Raḥīm.
In the name of Allah, the Most Merciful, the Especially Merciful.

Surah al-Fatihah

الْحَمْدُ لِلّٰهِ رَبِّ الْعَالَمِينَ
الرَّحْمٰنِ الرَّحِيمِ
مَالِكِ يَوْمِ الدِّينِ
إِيَّاكَ نَعْبُدُ وَإِيَّاكَ نَسْتَعِينُ
اهْدِنَا الصِّرَاطَ الْمُسْتَقِيمَ
صِرَاطَ الَّذِينَ أَنْعَمْتَ عَلَيْهِمْ
غَيْرِ الْمَغْضُوبِ عَلَيْهِمْ وَلَا الضَّالِّينَ
Al-ḥamdu lillāhi Rabbil-‘ālamīn. Ar-Raḥmānir-Raḥīm. Māliki yawmid-dīn. Iyyāka na‘budu wa iyyāka nasta‘īn. Ihdinaṣ-ṣirāṭal-mustaqīm. Ṣirāṭallaḏīna an‘amta ‘alayhim. Ghayril-maghḍūbi ‘alayhim wa laḍ-ḍāllīn.
All praise is for Allah, Lord of the worlds, the Most Merciful, the Especially Merciful, Master of the Day of Judgement. You alone we worship and You alone we ask for help. Guide us to the straight path, the path of those whom You have favoured, not of those who earned anger nor of those who went astray.

Aameen

آمِين
Āmīn.
O Allah, answer.

For one who cannot yet memorise al-Fatihah

سُبْحَانَ اللّٰهِ وَالْحَمْدُ لِلّٰهِ وَلَا إِلٰهَ إِلَّا اللّٰهُ وَاللّٰهُ أَكْبَرُ وَلَا حَوْلَ وَلَا قُوَّةَ إِلَّا بِاللّٰهِ
Subḥānallāhi wal-ḥamdu lillāhi wa lā ilāha illallāhu wallāhu akbar wa lā ḥawla wa lā quwwata illā billāh.
Glory be to Allah, praise belongs to Allah, there is no god but Allah, Allah is the Greatest, and there is no power and no might except with Allah.

After completing the recitation, the Prophet ﷺ would pause, raise his hands, say takbeer, and bow into rukoo‘. He placed his hands firmly on his knees, spread his fingers, straightened his back, and kept his head level. He warned against rushing in rukoo‘ and sujood.

Hadeeth meaning

The Prophet ﷺ said that the worst thief is the one who steals from his prayer by not completing his rukoo‘ or sujood. This teaches that calmness and completeness are essential.

Recitation 1

سُبْحَانَ رَبِّيَ الْعَظِيمِ
Subḥāna Rabbiyal-‘Aẓīm.
Glory be to my Lord, the Magnificent.

Recitation 2

سُبْحَانَ رَبِّيَ الْعَظِيمِ وَبِحَمْدِهِ
Subḥāna Rabbiyal-‘Aẓīmi wa biḥamdih.
Glory be to my Lord, the Magnificent, and praise belongs to Him.

Recitation 3

سُبُّوحٌ قُدُّوسٌ رَبُّ الْمَلَائِكَةِ وَالرُّوحِ
Subbūḥun Quddūs, Rabbul-malā’ikati war-rūḥ.
The Most Glorified, the Most Holy, Lord of the angels and the Spirit.

Recitation 4

اللّٰهُمَّ لَكَ رَكَعْتُ وَبِكَ آمَنْتُ وَلَكَ أَسْلَمْتُ وَعَلَيْكَ تَوَكَّلْتُ أَنْتَ رَبِّي خَشَعَ سَمْعِي وَبَصَرِي وَدَمِي وَلَحْمِي وَعَظْمِي وَعَصَبِي لِلّٰهِ رَبِّ الْعَالَمِينَ
Allāhumma laka raka‘tu wa bika āmantu wa laka aslamtu wa ‘alayka tawakkaltu anta Rabbī, khasha‘a sam‘ī wa baṣarī wa damī wa laḥmī wa ‘aẓmī wa ‘aṣabī lillāhi Rabbil-‘ālamīn.
O Allah, for You I have bowed, in You I have believed, to You I have submitted, and upon You I have relied. My hearing, sight, blood, flesh, bones, and nerves are humbled to Allah, Lord of the worlds.
The text also states that the Qur’an is not to be recited in rukoo‘.

When rising from rukoo‘, the Prophet ﷺ would raise his hands and say “Sami‘allāhu liman ḥamidah.” After standing completely upright, he would praise Allah. A person should not move into sujood before standing upright and pausing properly.

Recitation when rising

سَمِعَ اللّٰهُ لِمَنْ حَمِدَهُ
Sami‘allāhu liman ḥamidah.
Allah hears the one who praises Him.

Recitation after standing

رَبَّنَا لَكَ الْحَمْدُ
رَبَّنَا وَلَكَ الْحَمْدُ
اللّٰهُمَّ رَبَّنَا وَلَكَ الْحَمْدُ
Rabbanā lakal-ḥamd. Rabbanā wa lakal-ḥamd. Allāhumma Rabbanā wa lakal-ḥamd.
Our Lord, to You belongs all praise. / Our Lord, and to You belongs all praise. / O Allah, our Lord, and to You belongs all praise.

Additional praise

مِلْءَ السَّمَاوَاتِ وَمِلْءَ الْأَرْضِ وَمِلْءَ مَا شِئْتَ مِنْ شَيْءٍ بَعْدُ
Mil’as-samāwāti wa mil’al-arḍi wa mil’a mā shi’ta min shay’in ba‘d.
Filling the heavens, filling the earth, and filling whatever else You wish after that.

After the standing position following rukoo‘, the Prophet ﷺ would say takbeer and go into sujood. In sujood, he placed the forehead and nose firmly on the ground, kept the arms away from the sides, lifted the forearms off the floor, and pointed the toes towards the qiblah. After the first sajdah, he sat between the two sajdahs before returning to sujood again.

What is juloos?

Juloos means sitting in prayer. In this section it refers to the sitting between the two sajdahs.

What is iftiraash?

Iftiraash is to sit on the left foot while keeping the right foot upright, with its toes pointing towards the qiblah. This is the common form of sitting between the two sajdahs and in the first tashahhud.

What is iq‘aa’?

Iq‘aa’ is another reported form of sitting in which one sits on the heels with the feet upright and toes pointing towards the qiblah.

Hadeeth meaning

The text states that there is no prayer for the one whose nose does not touch the ground as the forehead does. It also warns against spreading the forearms on the floor like a dog.

Recitation in sujood 1

سُبْحَانَ رَبِّيَ الْأَعْلَى
Subḥāna Rabbiyal-A‘lā.
Glory be to my Lord, the Most High.

Recitation in sujood 2

سُبُّوحٌ قُدُّوسٌ رَبُّ الْمَلَائِكَةِ وَالرُّوحِ
Subbūḥun Quddūs, Rabbul-malā’ikati war-rūḥ.
The Most Glorified, the Most Holy, Lord of the angels and the Spirit.

Recitation in sujood 3

اللّٰهُمَّ لَكَ سَجَدْتُ وَبِكَ آمَنْتُ وَلَكَ أَسْلَمْتُ، سَجَدَ وَجْهِيَ لِلَّذِي خَلَقَهُ وَصَوَّرَهُ وَشَقَّ سَمْعَهُ وَبَصَرَهُ، تَبَارَكَ اللّٰهُ أَحْسَنُ الْخَالِقِينَ
Allāhumma laka sajadtu wa bika āmantu wa laka aslamtu. Sajada wajhī lilladhī khalaqahu wa ṣawwarahu wa shaqqa sam‘ahu wa baṣarahu, tabārakallāhu aḥsanul-khāliqīn.
O Allah, for You I have prostrated, in You I have believed, and to You I have submitted. My face has prostrated to the One who created it, formed it, and opened its hearing and sight. Blessed is Allah, the Best of creators.

Recitation in sujood 4

اللّٰهُمَّ إِنِّي أَعُوذُ بِرِضَاكَ مِنْ سَخَطِكَ وَبِمُعَافَاتِكَ مِنْ عُقُوبَتِكَ وَأَعُوذُ بِكَ مِنْكَ، لَا أُحْصِي ثَنَاءً عَلَيْكَ، أَنْتَ كَمَا أَثْنَيْتَ عَلَى نَفْسِكَ
Allāhumma innī a‘ūdhu biriḍāka min sakhaṭik, wa bimu‘āfātika min ‘uqūbatik, wa a‘ūdhu bika minka, lā uḥṣī thanā’an ‘alayk, anta kamā athnayta ‘alā nafsik.
O Allah, I seek refuge in Your pleasure from Your anger, in Your protection from Your punishment, and in You from You. I cannot fully praise You; You are as You have praised Yourself.

Recitation while sitting between the two sajdahs 1

اللّٰهُمَّ اغْفِرْ لِي وَارْحَمْنِي وَعَافِنِي وَاهْدِنِي وَارْزُقْنِي
Allāhum-maghfir lī warḥamnī wa ‘āfinī wahdinī وارزقني.
O Allah, forgive me, have mercy on me, grant me well-being, guide me, and provide for me.

Recitation while sitting between the two sajdahs 2

رَبِّ اغْفِرْ لِي رَبِّ اغْفِرْ لِي
Rabbighfir lī, Rabbighfir lī.
My Lord, forgive me; my Lord, forgive me.

After the second sujood of the second rak‘ah, the Prophet ﷺ would sit in iftiraash for the first tashahhud. He placed the left hand on the left knee, made a fist or circle with the fingers of the right hand, and pointed with the index finger while making du‘aa.

What is iftiraash?

Iftiraash is to sit on the left foot while the right foot remains upright with the toes facing the qiblah. This sitting is used in the first tashahhud.

Hadeeth meaning

The Prophet ﷺ taught the tashahhud to the companions as carefully as he taught them a surah from the Qur’an. This shows its importance and precision.

Tashahhud

التَّحِيَّاتُ لِلّٰهِ وَالصَّلَوَاتُ وَالطَّيِّبَاتُ، السَّلَامُ عَلَيْكَ أَيُّهَا النَّبِيُّ وَرَحْمَةُ اللّٰهِ وَبَرَكَاتُهُ، السَّلَامُ عَلَيْنَا وَعَلَىٰ عِبَادِ اللّٰهِ الصَّالِحِينَ، أَشْهَدُ أَنْ لَا إِلٰهَ إِلَّا اللّٰهُ وَأَشْهَدُ أَنَّ مُحَمَّدًا عَبْدُهُ وَرَسُولُهُ
At-taḥiyyātu lillāhi waṣ-ṣalawātu waṭ-ṭayyibāt. As-salāmu ‘alayka ayyuhan-nabiyyu wa raḥmatullāhi wa barakātuh. As-salāmu ‘alaynā wa ‘alā ‘ibādillāhiṣ-ṣāliḥīn. Ashhadu an lā ilāha illallāh wa ashhadu anna Muḥammadan ‘abduhu wa rasūluh.
All greetings, prayers, and pure words are for Allah. Peace be upon you, O Prophet, and the mercy of Allah and His blessings. Peace be upon us and upon the righteous servants of Allah. I testify that there is no god except Allah and I testify that Muhammad is His servant and Messenger.

After the tashahhud, the Prophet ﷺ taught his companions to send salāh upon him in different authentic forms. These are all acceptable. A student should memorise one carefully and understand its meaning.

Form 1

اللّٰهُمَّ صَلِّ عَلَىٰ مُحَمَّدٍ وَعَلَىٰ آلِ مُحَمَّدٍ كَمَا صَلَّيْتَ عَلَىٰ إِبْرَاهِيمَ وَعَلَىٰ آلِ إِبْرَاهِيمَ إِنَّكَ حَمِيدٌ مَجِيدٌ، اللّٰهُمَّ بَارِكْ عَلَىٰ مُحَمَّدٍ وَعَلَىٰ آلِ مُحَمَّدٍ كَمَا بَارَكْتَ عَلَىٰ إِبْرَاهِيمَ وَعَلَىٰ آلِ إِبْرَاهِيمَ إِنَّكَ حَمِيدٌ مَجِيدٌ
Allāhumma ṣalli ‘alā Muḥammad wa ‘alā āli Muḥammad kamā ṣallayta ‘alā Ibrāhīm wa ‘alā āli Ibrāhīm innaka Ḥamīdun Majīd. Allāhumma bārik ‘alā Muḥammad wa ‘alā āli Muḥammad kamā bārakta ‘alā Ibrāhīm wa ‘alā āli Ibrāhīm innaka Ḥamīdun Majīd.
O Allah, send prayers upon Muhammad and the family of Muhammad as You sent prayers upon Ibrahim and the family of Ibrahim. Indeed, You are Praiseworthy and Glorious. O Allah, bless Muhammad and the family of Muhammad as You blessed Ibrahim and the family of Ibrahim. Indeed, You are Praiseworthy and Glorious.

Form 2

اللّٰهُمَّ صَلِّ عَلَىٰ مُحَمَّدٍ عَبْدِكَ وَرَسُولِكَ كَمَا صَلَّيْتَ عَلَىٰ آلِ إِبْرَاهِيمَ، وَبَارِكْ عَلَىٰ مُحَمَّدٍ عَبْدِكَ وَرَسُولِكَ وَعَلَىٰ آلِ مُحَمَّدٍ كَمَا بَارَكْتَ عَلَىٰ إِبْرَاهِيمَ وَعَلَىٰ آلِ إِبْرَاهِيمَ
Allāhumma ṣalli ‘alā Muḥammad ‘abdika wa rasūlika kamā ṣallayta ‘alā āli Ibrāhīm, wa bārik ‘alā Muḥammad ‘abdika wa rasūlika wa ‘alā āli Muḥammad kamā bārakta ‘alā Ibrāhīm wa ‘alā āli Ibrāhīm.
O Allah, send prayers upon Muhammad, Your servant and Messenger, as You sent prayers upon the family of Ibrahim; and bless Muhammad, Your servant and Messenger, and the family of Muhammad, as You blessed Ibrahim and the family of Ibrahim.

After the second rak‘ah, the Prophet ﷺ would stand for the third rak‘ah with takbeer. In the final sitting, he would sit differently from the first tashahhud. This final sitting is called tawarruq.

What is tawarruq?

Tawarruq is the sitting of the final tashahhud in prayers with more than two rak‘ahs. In this position, the person sits on the ground with the left leg placed under the right side, while the right foot remains upright or is sometimes laid to one side. This makes it different from iftiraash.

Difference between iftiraash and tawarruq

Iftiraash is used in the first tashahhud and often between the two sajdahs; the person sits on the left foot. Tawarruq is used in the last tashahhud; the person sits more deeply on the ground with the left leg coming out from under the body.

Seeking refuge from the trials

اللّٰهُمَّ إِنِّي أَعُوذُ بِكَ مِنْ عَذَابِ جَهَنَّمَ وَمِنْ عَذَابِ الْقَبْرِ وَمِنْ فِتْنَةِ الْمَحْيَا وَالْمَمَاتِ وَمِنْ شَرِّ فِتْنَةِ الْمَسِيحِ الدَّجَّالِ
Allāhumma innī a‘ūdhu bika min ‘adhābi Jahannam wa min ‘adhābil-qabr wa min fitnatil-maḥyā wal-mamāt wa min sharri fitnatil-Masīḥid-Dajjāl.
O Allah, I seek refuge in You from the punishment of Hell, the punishment of the grave, the trials of life and death, and the evil trial of al-Masih ad-Dajjal.

Another form

اللّٰهُمَّ إِنِّي أَعُوذُ بِكَ مِنْ عَذَابِ الْقَبْرِ وَأَعُوذُ بِكَ مِنْ فِتْنَةِ الْمَسِيحِ الدَّجَّالِ وَأَعُوذُ بِكَ مِنْ فِتْنَةِ الْمَحْيَا وَالْمَمَاتِ، اللّٰهُمَّ إِنِّي أَعُوذُ بِكَ مِنَ الْمَأْثَمِ وَالْمَغْرَمِ
Allāhumma innī a‘ūdhu bika min ‘adhābil-qabr wa a‘ūdhu bika min fitnatil-Masīḥid-Dajjāl wa a‘ūdhu bika min fitnatil-maḥyā wal-mamāt. Allāhumma innī a‘ūdhu bika minal-ma’thami wal-maghram.
O Allah, I seek refuge in You from the punishment of the grave, from the trial of al-Masih ad-Dajjal, from the trial of life and death, and I seek refuge in You from sin and debt.

Before ending the prayer, the Prophet ﷺ would say different supplications. He also encouraged the companions to make du‘aa before tasleem. This is an important point in the prayer for personal supplication.

Du‘aa taught to Abu Bakr رضي الله عنه

اللّٰهُمَّ إِنِّي ظَلَمْتُ نَفْسِي ظُلْمًا كَثِيرًا وَلَا يَغْفِرُ الذُّنُوبَ إِلَّا أَنْتَ فَاغْفِرْ لِي مَغْفِرَةً مِنْ عِنْدِكَ وَارْحَمْنِي إِنَّكَ أَنْتَ الْغَفُورُ الرَّحِيمُ
Allāhumma innī ẓalamtu nafsī ẓulman kathīrā wa lā yaghfirudh-dhunūba illā anta, faghfir lī maghfiratan min ‘indik وارحمني innaka anta al-Ghafūrur-Raḥīm.
O Allah, I have greatly wronged myself, and none forgives sins except You, so forgive me with a forgiveness from You and have mercy on me. Surely You are the Most Forgiving, the Most Merciful.

Another du‘aa before tasleem

اللّٰهُمَّ اغْفِرْ لِي مَا قَدَّمْتُ وَمَا أَخَّرْتُ وَمَا أَسْرَرْتُ وَمَا أَعْلَنْتُ وَمَا أَسْرَفْتُ وَمَا أَنْتَ أَعْلَمُ بِهِ مِنِّي، أَنْتَ الْمُقَدِّمُ وَأَنْتَ الْمُؤَخِّرُ لَا إِلٰهَ إِلَّا أَنْتَ
Allāhummaghfir lī mā qaddamtu wa mā akhkhartu wa mā asrartu wa mā a‘lantu wa mā asraftu wa mā anta a‘lamu bihi minnī. Antal-Muqaddimu wa antal-Mu’akhkhiru lā ilāha illā anta.
O Allah, forgive me for what I have done before and what I have delayed, what I have hidden and what I have made public, what I have exceeded in, and what You know better than I. You are the One who brings forward and the One who puts back. There is no god except You.

The Prophet ﷺ ended the prayer by giving tasleem to the right and left. Different authentic forms are reported. The lesson text also mentions that he corrected those who waved their hands while making tasleem.

Hadeeth meaning

The Prophet ﷺ told the companions that it is enough to keep the hand on the thigh and give salaam to the right and left. Tasleem is not done with hand-waving.

Form 1

السَّلَامُ عَلَيْكُمْ وَرَحْمَةُ اللّٰهِ وَبَرَكَاتُهُ
As-salāmu ‘alaykum wa raḥmatullāhi wa barakātuh.
Peace, the mercy of Allah, and His blessings be upon you.

Form 2

السَّلَامُ عَلَيْكُمْ وَرَحْمَةُ اللّٰهِ
As-salāmu ‘alaykum wa raḥmatullāh.
Peace and the mercy of Allah be upon you.

Shorter form

السَّلَامُ عَلَيْكُمْ
As-salāmu ‘alaykum.
Peace be upon you.
