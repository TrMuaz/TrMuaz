Zakat Interactive Lesson – Zakat al-Māl & Zakat al-Fitr

With Chinese translation (中文) and pinyin (拼音) for classroom use.

1. Lesson Objectives & Warm-up

Lesson objectives

  • Define zakat and explain its purpose (pillar of Islam, obligatory charity).
  • Distinguish zakat al-māl and zakat al-fitr (object, timing, amount, wisdom).
  • Use basic Mandarin terms about zakat with correct pinyin.

2. What is Zakat?

Short explanation

Zakat is a pillar of Islam, an obligatory charity that purifies a Muslim’s wealth and supports those in need.

It is different from voluntary charity (sadaqah), because zakat has fixed rules, conditions and specific recipients.

Bilingual key sentence

Zakat is an obligatory charity in Islam.
伊斯兰教里的天课是强制性的施舍。
Yīsīlán jiào lǐ de tiān kè shì qiángzhì xìng de shīshě.

3. Zakat al-Māl (天课：财产)

Core idea

Zakat al-māl is zakat on wealth such as cash, gold and business assets that are held for one lunar year and reach the nisab threshold.

The common rate is 2.5% of eligible wealth. Its goal is to purify extra wealth and support the poor and other eligible groups.

Bilingual definition

Zakat al-māl is obligatory charity on a Muslim’s wealth when it reaches a certain minimum amount.
财产天课是当穆斯林的财产达到一定数额时必须交付的施舍。
Cáichǎn tiān kè shì dāng Mùsīlín de cáichǎn dádào yīdìng shù’é shí bìxū jiāofù de shīshě.

Mini calculation example

Ali has RM 10,000 in savings for one lunar year, above nisab. The zakat al-māl rate is 2.5%.

Question (English): How much zakat must he pay?
问题 (中文): 你认为阿里应该交多少天课？
Nǐ rènwéi Ālǐ yīnggāi jiāo duōshǎo tiān kè?

4. Zakat al-Fitr / Fitrah (开斋天课)

Core idea

Zakat al-fitr is an obligatory charity given at the end of Ramadan, before the Eid al-Fitr prayer.

It is paid for every Muslim in the household (self and dependents), usually as a fixed amount of staple food or its cash value.

It purifies the fasting person and helps the poor celebrate Eid.

Bilingual definition

Zakat al-fitr is an obligatory charity paid at the end of Ramadan for each Muslim, mainly in staple food.
开斋天课是在斋月结束时，为每个穆斯林所交的一种施舍，多数以主食的形式给出。
Kāizhāi tiān kè shì zài zhāiyuè jiéshù shí, wèi měi gè Mùsīlín suǒ jiāo de yī zhǒng shīshě, duōshù yǐ zhǔshí de xíngshì gěichū.

Note: “开斋天课” (kāizhāi tiān kè) here is a teaching term for “zakat for breaking the fast”.

5. Compare Zakat al-Māl & Zakat al-Fitr

Key differences table

Aspect Zakat al-māl
天课（财产）		 Zakat al-fitr
开斋天课
Main object Wealth (money, gold, business assets). Staple food (e.g. rice) or its cash value.
When paid Once every lunar year when wealth reaches nisab. At the end of Ramadan, before Eid al-Fitr prayer.
Basis of amount Percentage of wealth (often 2.5%). Fixed amount per person.
Who must pay Muslims whose wealth is above nisab. Every Muslim who has enough food for Eid, including dependents.
Main wisdom (hikmah) Purify wealth, reduce inequality. Purify fasting, help the poor enjoy Eid.

Group practice: situation cards

Instructions for students:

  1. Read the situation.
  2. Decide: zakat al-māl or zakat al-fitr?
  3. Answer in English and Chinese.

Example answers:

  • 这是财产天课。 – Zhè shì cáichǎn tiān kè. (This is zakat al-māl.)
  • 这是开斋天课。 – Zhè shì kāizhāi tiān kè. (This is zakat al-fitr.)

Quick practice (MCQ)

“A family wants to pay for 5 members before the Eid al-Fitr prayer.” Which zakat is this?

6. Language Focus – Sentences

Read and repeat

  1. Zakat al-māl is zakat on wealth.
    财产天课是对财产征收的天课。
    Cáichǎn tiān kè shì duì cáichǎn zhēngshōu de tiān kè.
  2. Zakat al-fitr is paid at the end of Ramadan.
    开斋天课在斋月结束时交付。
    Kāizhāi tiān kè zài zhāiyuè jiéshù shí jiāofù.
  3. Zakat helps the poor and purifies our wealth.
    天课帮助贫穷的人，也净化我们的财产。
    Tiān kè bāngzhù pínqióng de rén, yě jìnghuà wǒmen de cáichǎn.
  4. Every Muslim who has enough wealth must pay zakat al-māl.
    每个有足够财产的穆斯林都必须交财产天课。
    Měi gè yǒu zúgòu cáichǎn de Mùsīlín dōu bìxū jiāo cáichǎn tiān kè.
  5. Every Muslim should pay zakat al-fitr for himself and his family.
    每个穆斯林都应该为自己和家人交开斋天课。
    Měi gè Mùsīlín dōu yīnggāi wèi zìjǐ hé jiārén jiāo kāizhāi tiān kè.

Simple dialogue idea

Student A: 你交了天课吗？
Nǐ jiāo le tiān kè ma? (Have you paid zakat?)

Student B: 我已经交了开斋天课。
Wǒ yǐjīng jiāo le kāizhāi tiān kè. (I have already paid zakat al-fitr.)
