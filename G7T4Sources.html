Source 1

Al-Qur'an

The Word of Allah ﷻ — the #1 Source
What is the Qur'an?
The Qur'an is the direct Word of Allah ﷻ, revealed to Prophet Muhammad ﷺ through the Angel Jibreel (Gabriel). It is the number one source of Islamic law — everything else must agree with it!
🕰️
Revealed over 23 years — from when the Prophet ﷺ was 40 years old until he passed away.
🌟
It is a miracle — no human could produce anything like it. Allah ﷻ challenged the Arabs (who were masters of poetry!) to make just one chapter like it.
🔒
100% preserved — the Qur'an has not changed a single letter since it was revealed. Millions have memorised it completely!
📜
114 Surahs (chapters) and over 6,000 verses, covering belief, worship, law, stories, and guidance for life.
💬
Originally in Arabic — the language was so perfectly chosen that it is still a living language today, used by over 400 million people.
Why is the Qur'an a Miracle?
🎯
Allah ﷻ challenged all of humanity to produce something like the Qur'an — and nobody has succeeded in 1,400+ years! This challenge (called al-Tahaddi) is still open today.
وَإِن كُنتُمْ فِي رَيْبٍ مِّمَّا نَزَّلْنَا عَلَىٰ عَبْدِنَا فَأْتُوا بِسُورَةٍ مِّن مِّثْلِهِ
"And if you are in doubt about what We have revealed to Our servant, then produce a surah (chapter) like it..."
📍 Surah Al-Baqarah (2:23)

The Arabs of Makkah were experts in poetry and language. They would compete in giant poetry contests. Yet when Allah ﷻ challenged them to produce just ONE surah like the Qur'an — they couldn't! Instead, they chose to fight wars against the Muslims. That's how impossible the challenge was. 💪

🌊
Barrier between seas: The Qur'an mentions that two seas meet but don't mix. Modern science confirmed this — different densities create a thin barrier! (Surah Al-Rahman 55:19-20)
مَرَجَ الْبَحْرَيْنِ يَلْتَقِيَانِ بَيْنَهُمَا بَرْزَخٌ لَّا يَبْغِيَانِ
"He released the two seas, meeting [side by side]; Between them is a barrier [so] neither of them transgresses."
📍 Surah Al-Rahman (55:19-20)
🌍
Expanding Universe: Scientists only discovered the universe is expanding in 1929. The Qur'an mentioned it 1,400 years ago! (Surah Adh-Dhariyat 51:47)
🤰
Human embryo stages: The Qur'an describes how a baby forms in stages — confirmed by modern embryology! (Surah Al-Mu'minoon 23:12-14)
✍️
The literary style of the Qur'an is totally unique — it's not poetry, not prose, not rhyme — it's in its own category that Arab language experts have never been able to copy.
إِنَّا أَعْطَيْنَاكَ الْكَوْثَرَ فَصَلِّ لِرَبِّكَ وَانْحَرْ إِنَّ شَانِئَكَ هُوَ الْأَبْتَرُ
"Indeed, We have granted you, [O Muhammad], al-Kawthar. So pray to your Lord and sacrifice [to Him alone]. Indeed, your enemy is the one cut off."
📍 Surah Al-Kawthar (108:1-3) — the shortest surah, yet perfect in rhyme, rhythm & meaning

Even the short surahs have a perfect internal structure — every word fits exactly, carries deep meaning, AND sounds beautiful when recited. No human author has matched this.

Laws Derived from the Qur'an

The Qur'an directly sets many Islamic laws. Here are two important ones:

The Qur'an gives exact fractions for who gets what from a person's wealth when they die. This is called Ilm al-Faraa'id (the science of inheritance). For example:

يُوصِيكُمُ اللَّهُ فِي أَوْلَادِكُمْ ۖ لِلذَّكَرِ مِثْلُ حَظِّ الْأُنثَيَيْنِ
"Allah instructs you regarding your children: for the male, what is equal to the share of two females..."
📍 Surah An-Nisaa' (4:11)
👨 Son = 2x daughter's share 👩 Daughter = ½ of son's share 👨‍👩‍👧 Parents each get 1/6
Quick example: If a man leaves behind RM 60,000 and has one son and one daughter — the son gets RM 40,000 (2 parts) and the daughter gets RM 20,000 (1 part). This is exact and fair according to Islamic law.
وَالسَّارِقُ وَالسَّارِقَةُ فَاقْطَعُوا أَيْدِيَهُمَا جَزَاءً بِمَا كَسَبَا نَكَالًا مِّنَ اللَّهِ
"As for the thief, the male and the female, cut off their hands as recompense for what they committed as a deterrent [punishment] from Allah..."
📍 Surah Al-Maa'idah (5:38)
⚠️
This verse sets the maximum punishment for theft. But it only applies when very strict conditions are met (you'll learn those in the Sunnah section!) — Islam is very careful before applying this.
🕌
Source 2

Sunnah & Hadeeth

The Prophet's ﷺ sayings, actions & approvals
What is the Sunnah?
Sunnah means "the way" or "practice" of Prophet Muhammad ﷺ. It is the second source of Islamic law. Whatever the Prophet ﷺ said, did, or allowed is part of the Sunnah.
Hadeeth means "narration" — these are the written records of the Prophet's ﷺ Sunnah. Scholars worked very hard to collect and verify them to make sure they are authentic (real).
🔢
The Sunnah has 3 types: Qawli (sayings), Fi'li (actions), and Taqriri (approvals). Click the "3 Types" tab to explore each one!
📚
Why do we follow the Sunnah? Allah ﷻ commands us to: "Obey Allah and obey the Messenger" (Surah An-Nisaa' 4:59)
🔍
It explains the Qur'an — the Qur'an says "establish prayer" but doesn't tell us HOW. The Sunnah shows us exactly how to pray.
The 3 Types of Sunnah
💬 1. Qawli — Sayings Words

Things the Prophet ﷺ said — these are direct instructions or advice in his own words.

"The best of you is the one who learned the Qur'an and taught it to others."
📍 Hadeeth — Saheeh Al-Bukhari #5027
"Actions are judged by their intentions, and every person will get what they intended."
📍 Hadeeth — Saheeh Al-Bukhari #1
🤲 2. Fi'li — Actions Deeds

Things the Prophet ﷺ did — Muslims follow his example in how he prayed, fasted, dressed, etc.

Example: The Prophet ﷺ prayed Salaah a specific way — with exact movements, positions, and words. His companions watched him and wrote it down. That's how we know how to pray today!
Example: The Prophet ﷺ used to eat with his right hand and start from the edge of the plate, not the middle. Muslims follow this sunnah of eating.
✅ 3. Taqriri — Approvals Silent OK

Things the Prophet ﷺ saw or heard a companion doing but did NOT correct. His silence means it was OK!

Example: A companion was eating a lizard in front of the Prophet ﷺ. The Prophet ﷺ didn't eat it himself, but he also didn't stop the companion. His silence = approval. Therefore eating lizard is permissible (though not everyone's favourite! 🦎).
Laws Derived from the Sunnah

The Sunnah fills in the details that the Qur'an doesn't mention. Here are two examples:

"A Muslim does not inherit from a non-Muslim, and a non-Muslim does not inherit from a Muslim."
📍 Hadeeth — Saheeh Al-Bukhari #6764
🚫
A murderer cannot inherit from the person they killed. The Qur'an didn't mention this — the Prophet ﷺ clarified it in his Sunnah.
Why is this important? Without the Sunnah, people could misuse the Qur'anic inheritance laws. The Sunnah protects fairness!

The Qur'an says the punishment for theft is cutting the hand. But how much must be stolen before this applies? The Sunnah tells us!

"The hand is cut off for [stealing] a quarter of a dinar or more."
📍 Hadeeth — Saheeh Al-Bukhari #6789
💰 Nisaab = ¼ dinar of gold ≈ About 1.0625 grams of gold ≈ Approximately RM 730+ today
🧠
If someone steals less than the nisaab (minimum amount), the severe punishment does NOT apply. Islam is very precise and just in how punishments are applied!
🤝
Source 3

Ijmaa'

Unanimous agreement of the Sahaabah
What is Ijmaa'?
Ijmaa' (إجماع) means "agreement" or "consensus". It is the joint agreement of the Sahaabah (the Companions of the Prophet ﷺ) on a point of Islamic law that is NOT directly found in the Qur'an or Sunnah — but is based on them and does not contradict them.
👥
Who are the Sahaabah? They are the companions who lived with the Prophet ﷺ, heard him, saw him, and learned directly from him. They are the most trustworthy people in Islamic history.
📅
When is it used? After the Prophet ﷺ passed away, new situations arose that were not directly covered. The Sahaabah would gather and figure out the ruling together.
⚡
Why is it important? It shows that Islam can adapt to new situations in all times and places, while staying true to the Qur'an and Sunnah.
🔗
It's always secondary — Ijmaa' never overrides the Qur'an or Sunnah. It only fills gaps where direct guidance wasn't given.
How Does Ijmaa' Work?
🏛️
Imagine the leaders and scholars of the Muslim community sitting together to solve a new problem. Here's the step-by-step process:
1
A new problem arises that Muslims haven't faced before (e.g., a new law, a new situation in society).
2
The Caliph (Muslim leader) gathers the knowledgeable Sahaabah together.
3
They search the Qur'an — did Allah ﷻ say anything about this? If yes → done! If not → next step.
4
They check the Sunnah — did the Prophet ﷺ say or do anything about this? If yes → done! If not → next step.
5
Everyone gives their opinion based on Islamic principles. The opinions are discussed and debated.
6
They reach full agreement — the best option becomes the new law for all Muslims.
Think of it like this: It's like a school assembly where all the teachers and principals discuss a new school rule. They can't make a rule that breaks the school constitution (Qur'an) or the headmaster's guidelines (Sunnah) — but for new issues, they work together!
Famous Examples of Ijmaa'

The Qur'an was revealed in pieces over 23 years. After the Prophet ﷺ passed away, parts were written on palm leaves, animal bones, and tree bark. Many companions had memorised it, but there was no single book!

⚔️
Many Huffaaz (Qur'an memorisers) were killed in the Battle of Yamaamah under Caliph Abu Bakr ؓ.
🤝
The Sahaabah agreed by Ijmaa' to collect all pieces into ONE official book.
✍️
Zayd ibn Thaabit ؓ was chosen for the task — he was the Prophet's ﷺ personal scribe and had memorised the entire Qur'an.
✅
He collected all written pieces, compared them with what people had memorised, and produced the official Mushaf (written Qur'an).
💡
This is why today, every Qur'an in the world — in Malaysia, Saudi Arabia, America, Indonesia — has the exact same text. This was made possible by Ijmaa'!
In the Prophet's ﷺ time: 1 Adhaan

During the Prophet's ﷺ time, there was only ONE Adhaan for Jumu'ah (Friday prayer). It was called when the Prophet ﷺ entered the mosque and said "Assalamu Alaykum". During Abu Bakr ؓ and Umar ؓ, it stayed the same.

During Caliph Uthmaan ibn Affaan ؓ, the city of Madeenah had grown very large, and the marketplace was so noisy that people couldn't hear the Adhaan in time!

🏙️
Problem: Market noise drowned out the Friday Adhaan.
💡
Uthmaan ؓ suggested adding a second early Adhaan in the marketplace before the main one.
✅
All the Sahaabah agreed — this became law by Ijmaa'.
Now: 2 Adhaans for Jumu'ah
This is why in many mosques today, there are two Adhaans before the Friday Khutbah (sermon)!
⚖️
Source 4

Qiyaas

Analogical reasoning — Islam & modern life
What is Qiyaas?
Qiyaas (قياس) means "analogy" or "comparison". It is the 4th and last source of Islamic law. When a new situation arises that isn't in the Qur'an, Sunnah, or Ijmaa', scholars compare it to a similar existing ruling and apply the same reason (cause) to figure out the ruling.
🧠
Think of it like a logic puzzle: If A = B, and C is similar to A, then C = B. We use the same reason to reach the same conclusion.
🌍
Why is it needed? Islam was revealed 1,400+ years ago. New things like smartphones, drugs, cars, internet, etc. didn't exist. Qiyaas helps scholars figure out their rulings!
⚠️
Only scholars can do Qiyaas — you need deep knowledge of the Qur'an, Sunnah, and Arabic language to correctly identify which rulings can be compared.
💡
Simple example: The Qur'an forbids alcohol (khamr) because it intoxicates. What about cocaine? It also intoxicates! So by Qiyaas, cocaine is also forbidden — same reason, same ruling. 🧪
The 4 Required Components of Qiyaas

For Qiyaas to be valid, 4 things must be present. Think of it like a formula! 🔬

📜
1. Al-Asl
The Original Case
The known ruling from Qur'an/Sunnah
e.g. Alcohol (khamr) is haram
🆕
2. Al-Far'
The New Case
The new situation needing a ruling
e.g. What about cocaine?
⚖️
3. Al-Hukm
The Ruling
The verdict applied to both cases
e.g. It is forbidden (Haram)
🔑
4. Al-'Illah
The Reason (Cause)
The shared reason linking both cases
e.g. It causes intoxication
🧮
The Qiyaas formula: If the new case shares the same 'Illah (reason/cause) as the original case, it gets the same ruling. The key is finding the correct 'Illah!
Real-Life Examples of Qiyaas

Drugs like marijuana and cocaine didn't exist in the Prophet's ﷺ time. So the Qur'an and Sunnah don't mention them by name. But the Prophet ﷺ said:

"Every intoxicant is Khamr, and every intoxicant is Haraam."
📍 Hadeeth — Saheeh Muslim #2003
"Whatever intoxicates in large amounts is Haraam in small amounts."
📍 Hadeeth — Abu Dawood #3681
Original (Asl)
Alcohol = Haram
New Case (Far')
Cocaine/Marijuana
Reason ('Illah)
Causes intoxication
Ruling (Hukm)
Also Haram! ❌

Cigarettes reached the Muslim world in the 17th century. At that time, the only known bad effect was bad breath. So scholars ruled it was Makrooh (disliked but not haram), based on a hadeeth about smelly food like garlic.

Now, modern medicine has proven that smoking causes cancer and can kill. This changes things! Look at what Allah ﷻ says:

وَلَا تَقْتُلُوا أَنفُسَكُمْ إِنَّ اللَّهَ كَانَ بِكُمْ رَحِيمًا
"And do not kill yourselves, for Allah is indeed merciful to you."
📍 Surah An-Nisaa' (4:29)
وَلَا تُلْقُوا بِأَيْدِيكُمْ إِلَى التَّهْلُكَةِ
"...and do not throw yourselves into destruction with your own hands."
📍 Surah Al-Baqarah (2:195)
17th century → Makrooh (disliked) Today → Haraam (forbidden)
🧠
This shows how Qiyaas can change as new knowledge is discovered. If taking small amounts of something slowly kills you, Islam says it's haram — same ruling as suicide.
